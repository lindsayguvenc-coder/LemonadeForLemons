The Situation

What's Happening to Our Counselors

The Moraga School District is one of the lowest-funded districts in the Bay Area — and without a new local funding source, 50% of student counseling positions are at risk of elimination. That includes credentialed clinicians like ours: licensed mental health professionals (LMFT, LPCC, PPSC) — the kind of support that builds healthier kids, stronger families, and a community worth living in.

The MSD Board is pursuing a parcel tax measure on the June 2, 2026 ballot — $295/year per parcel — that would protect counselors, instructional assistants, and teachers. Until that passes, our counselors' positions hang in the balance.

This campaign is one family's way of saying: we see what these people do, we know what's at stake, and we're not going to be quiet about it.

What Our Community Stands to Lose

When a school loses a counselor with extensive mental health credentials, it's not just one position that disappears. It's a layer of care and support that every student and family benefits from. She helps kids navigate anxiety, friendships, big emotions, and difficult moments so they can stay focused and feel safe at school. She is also someone teachers and parents can turn to when a child is struggling. Without that support, those needs don't go away. They simply become harder for the entire school community to manage.

🤖 Lindsay — AI Governance & Education Advocate

The Future of Education Needs This

The future learning environment is going to look very different from the one most of us grew up in. How content is delivered, how learning is paced, how mastery is measured — all of that is shifting.

What isn't shifting is this: a child who walks into a classroom carrying something heavy cannot learn. Not from any teacher, any tool, any method. The emotional and cognitive scaffolding layer isn't a nice-to-have alongside instruction. It's the precondition for instruction to work at all.

Hari Subramonyam's research at Stanford HAI makes this point structurally. As instructional delivery evolves, the human educator's role shifts toward holding the container, not just filling it. The relational, regulatory, emotionally present layer becomes more critical, not less, as everything else changes around it.

That layer has a name in most schools. It's the counselor.
